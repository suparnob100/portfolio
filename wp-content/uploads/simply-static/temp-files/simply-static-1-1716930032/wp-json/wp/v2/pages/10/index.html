I hold a Ph.D. in Engineering Science and Mechanics from Penn State University, an M.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur, and a BE in Mechanical Engineering from Jadavpur University, India.<\/p>\n

My doctoral research focused on the model order reduction of dynamical systems using Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD). This work produced a novel physics-informed criterion that effectively determined the dimension of reduced-order models, ensuring accurate representation for both linear and nonlinear structural vibration systems.<\/p>\n

After completing my doctoral studies, I undertook a postdoctoral position at Clemson University where I focused on topology optimization for bandgap maximization in 2D lattice structures and purely data-driven model discovery of structural vibration systems using neural ordinary differential equations.<\/p>\n

In August 2023, I transitioned to the TAMIDS (Texas A&M Institute of Data Science)<\/strong> Digital Twin Lab as a Postdoctoral Research Associate, working closely with Drs. Jian Tao, Jean Ragusa, and Eduardo Gildin on the reduced-order modeling of nonlinear systems.<\/p>\n

Outside of my research, I have earned certifications in machine learning and online teaching. I am also an Indian classical vocalist and enjoy traveling to experience diverse cultures and landscapes.<\/p>\n\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
